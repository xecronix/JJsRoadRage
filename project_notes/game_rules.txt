# Purpose: Simplified game rules for fast completion (FBI Pursuit, forever level)

-----------------------------------------------------------------------
CORE CONSTANTS
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
- Tile size: 64x64 px
- Sprite size: 64x64 px (all actors)
- Level: 1 map, 1 level, runs forever (infinite scroll / looping world)
- Collision: axis-aligned bounding boxes (AABB)

Derived collision boxes:
- Default sprite box: 64x64
- Damage collision box: 62x62 (size - 5%)
- Bullet collision box: may be smaller than 62x62 (recommended)

-----------------------------------------------------------------------
ACTOR TYPES
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Player actors (2):
1) Hero
2) HeroBullet

NPC actors (4):
1) Enemy
2) EnemyBullet
3) Civilian
4) RoadSide (static or semi-static hazards/obstacles)

-----------------------------------------------------------------------
TILES AND TERRAIN DAMAGE (NEW)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Road layout:
- Roads are outlined in sand tiles.
- Grass is fully off-road.

Terrain damage rule (applies to all actors except bullets):
- If an actor bumps into (touches) a sand tile: take some damage.
- If an actor bumps into (touches) a grass tile: take max damage.
- Bullets do NOT attempt to stay on the road and are excluded from terrain steering.
  (Bullets may still be destroyed on impact with terrain later if desired, but not required.)

Definitions (needs one map legend decision):
- "Bumps into" means the actor's damage collision box overlaps a tile of that type,
  or the actor center point is over that tile. Pick one method and keep it consistent.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------
HERO (PLAYER) RULES
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Movement:
- Controls: Left, Right, Forward, Back, Shoot
- Hero cannot leave the screen (screen bounds clamp)
- Hero should stay on the road via terrain damage (sand hurts, grass hurts more)

Scoring:
- +score when HeroBullet destroys an Enemy
- -score when HeroBullet destroys a Civilian
- Objective: high score

Lives / Death:
- Game over when Hero dies 3 times
- Hero "dies" when hero.def <= 0
- On death: increment death counter, reset hero.def, respawn hero (position reset)

-----------------------------------------------------------------------
NPC POPULATION RULES
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
On-screen targets (minimums, tunable):
- At least 8 civilians on screen at all times
- At least 4 enemies on screen at all times
- These counts can be increased over time or by score thresholds (optional)

Respawn / Reset:
- Any NPC resets (respawns near top) when:
  - shot and destroyed (def <= 0), OR
  - travels off the bottom of the screen

Enemy-only firing rule:
- Enemy shoots at Hero when Hero is in line of fire (see Enemy Fire Rules)
- Enemy fires a maximum of 3 times before Enemy resets (shots fired, not hits)

Visual identity:
- Enemies are black and yellow cars
- Civilians are other distinct colors

-----------------------------------------------------------------------
NPC ROAD-FOLLOWING (UPDATED, SIMPLE STEERING)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Goal:
- NPCs remain on the road by tracking the left edge of the road (sand outline).

At NPC spawn:
- Determine a desired offset from the left edge of the road:
  - npc.desiredLeftOffsetPx is chosen at spawn time (configurable range).
- Each update tick:
  1) Find the left road boundary (sand tile) for the NPC's current row (or slightly ahead row).
  2) Compute current distance from NPC to that boundary.
  3) Adjust NPC.x by +/- 1 pixel to converge toward desiredLeftOffsetPx.

Important notes:
- This is steering, not a hard clamp. Terrain damage still applies if NPC hits sand/grass.
- Bullets do NOT use road-following logic.

Suggested robust "find left boundary" method:
- Convert NPC position to tile row.
- Scan tile columns from left to right until the first sand tile is found.
- LeftBoundaryXpx = colIndex * TILE_SIZE
- DesiredNpcXpx = LeftBoundaryXpx + npc.desiredLeftOffsetPx
- If npc.x < DesiredNpcXpx: npc.x += 1
  else if npc.x > DesiredNpcXpx: npc.x -= 1

Tunables:
- Scan row selection: same row vs 1-3 rows ahead (recommended: 1 row ahead)
- desiredLeftOffsetPx range by NPC type (enemies tighter, civilians looser)
- Steering step size (default: 1 px per update)

-----------------------------------------------------------------------
ENEMY FIRE RULES (LINE OF FIRE)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Line-of-fire check (simple v1):
- Enemy examines tile coordinate 3 rows ahead of itself (look-ahead).
- If the Hero is touching that coordinate (same lane / intersects that tile area), Enemy fires.
- EnemyBullet spawns from enemy position and travels toward the hero (or straight down lane).

Tunables:
- Look-ahead distance: 3 rows (can adjust)
- Fire cooldown: optional (recommended to avoid bullet spam)
- Max shots before reset: 3 (locked for now)

-----------------------------------------------------------------------
COLLISION AND DAMAGE RULES
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Collision method:
- Bounding box (AABB) overlap checks.

Damage application:
- On a damage-causing collision:
  actor.def -= other.atk

Suggested collision pairs that cause damage:
- Hero vs Enemy
- Hero vs EnemyBullet
- Enemy vs HeroBullet
- Civilian vs HeroBullet (causes civilian death and score penalty)
- Hero vs RoadSide (optional, if roadside hazards are damaging)

Terrain damage pairs (tile-based):
- Actor vs Sand tile: actor.def -= SAND_DMG (some damage)
- Actor vs Grass tile: actor.def -= GRASS_DMG (max damage)

- NPC counts: min civilians=8, min enemies=4
- Scroll speed
- Actor movement speed

- Enemy fire cooldown (if added)

- Scoring values (+enemy, -civilian)
